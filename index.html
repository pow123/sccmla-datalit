Data Information Literacy Competencies
New:
  • Data awareness and knowledge - understanding the role of data in research
  • Data responsibility - recognizing and minimizing bias and/or ambiguous or misleading representation in reporting
  • Data structuring and cleaning - preparing data for analysis
Modified:
  • Cultures of practice - changed from a competency to a component of each competency
  • Quality assurance → Data quality and documentation
  • Metadata → Data description

Example Competency - Ethics

Emerging

  • Develops understanding of attribution and reuse
  • Develops mindfulness in data ownership and privacy

Intermediate

  • Understands the ethos of the discipline for sharing and admin. of data
  • Recognizes the role of IP, privacy, and conf. issues in data collection and sharing

Expert

  • Creates data use agreements when needed
  • Anonymizes data & reduces risk of re-ID
Intermediate includes the learning outcomes listed under emerging, and expert includes those listed under emerging and intermediate.

Public Health Informatics

The course addresses the application of ethical principles that apply to the use of information systems as those pertain to accessing, collecting, analyzing, using, maintaining, and disseminating data and information.

  • Ethics
  • Responsibility
  • Discovery & acquisition
  • Management & organization
  • Structuring & cleaning
  • Visualization & representation

Activities

  • IRB training
  • Collecting data
  • Analyzing open data
  • Visualizing EHR data
